# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

ArVisual welcomes respectful technical collaboration from people of every background and experience level.

Examples of expected behavior include:

- communicating with patience, clarity, and professional respect;
- focusing criticism on code, evidence, and user impact rather than individuals;
- accepting constructive feedback and correcting mistakes openly;
- respecting privacy, attribution, licensing, and responsible disclosure;
- helping keep discussions useful to creators and contributors.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, personal attacks, threats, publishing private information, deliberate disruption, or repeated hostile conduct.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, release channels, and public spaces where someone is representing the ArVisual project.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock discussions, reject contributions, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation when conduct conflicts with this standard.

Report conduct concerns privately to `ari.sulistiono@gmail.com`. Include relevant links or screenshots and avoid sharing unrelated personal information. Reports will be reviewed as confidentially and fairly as practical.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the Contributor Covenant and adapted for the scale and workflow of the ArVisual project.
